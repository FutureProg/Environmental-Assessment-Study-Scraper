Manage Cookies

A cookie is information stored on your computer by a website you visit. Cookies often store your settings for a website, such as your preferred language or location. This allows the site to present you with information customized to fit your needs. As per the GDPR law, companies need to get your explicit approval to collect your data. Some of these cookies are ‘strictly necessary’ to provide the basic functions of the website and can not be turned off, while others if present, have the option of being turned off. Learn more about our Privacy and Cookie policies. These can be managed also from our cookie policy page.

  1. Strictly necessary cookies(always on):
    Necessary for enabling core functionality. The website cannot function properly without these cookies. This cannot be turned off. e.g. Sign in, Language
  2. Analytics cookies:
    Analytical cookies help us to analyse user behaviour, mainly to see if the users are able to find and act on things that they are looking for. They allow us to recognise and count the number of visitors and to see how visitors move around our website when they are using it. Tools used: Google Analytics

Burlington Transit

Share Burlington Transit on Facebook Share Burlington Transit on Twitter Share Burlington Transit on Linkedin Email Burlington Transit link

Burlington Transit has implemented several initiatives designed to provide equal access to transit services throughout the City.

Burlington Transit is the bus service run by the City of Burlington. We have buses with fixed routes and specialized transit systems for riders with special and mobility needs. Our routes connect to Hamilton, Oakville and the GO Transit routes.

    Please join us on Thursday, Feb. 16 for an informal open house at Burlington Public Library, Central Branch. We will be discussing Route 4 Central, with a focus on the service between downtown Burlington and the Aldershot GO Station.

    Date Thursday, Feb. 16, 2023

    Time 6 – 8 p.m. Formal presentation at 6:30 p.m.

    In-person Burlington Public Library, Central Branch, Centennial Hall, 2331 New St., Burlington

    Get there by bus! Route 4 Central can get you to 2331 New St., with a stop on New Street in front of the library. You can also catch a shuttle from the bus stop on Lasalle Park Road at North Shore Boulevard West or on Fairwood Place West at Aldershot School. To register for the shuttle, email contactbt@burlington.ca.

    Free parking is available onsite.
